🧠 Engineering Philosophy

How I Think About Systems

What separates production financial infrastructure from a demo is not features — it's rigour, paranoia, and the discipline to engineer for failure before engineering for success.

01
Secure-by-Design, Not Secure-by-Afterthought

Security is not a feature to bolt on — it's the structural foundation. Every system starts with a threat model. Cryptographic choices are explicitly justified: why AES-256-GCM over AES-CBC, why SHA-3 over SHA-2.

02
Financial Integrity is Non-Negotiable

Money is liability. Every design decision must prevent money from being lost, duplicated, or made inconsistent. INTEGER minor-unit storage and double-entry enforcement are foundations, not optimisations.

03
Explicit Over Implicit — Always

Autocommit databases in financial systems are a liability. Explicit transaction blocks with mandatory rollback make failure modes visible and auditable. ACID is a contract, and I honour it unconditionally.

04
Immutability as Audit Infrastructure

Append-only audit logs are the regulatory backbone of any compliant financial system. Combined with blockchain Merkle proofs, they create a tamper-evident record that survives insider threat scenarios.

05
Design for the Failure Case First

The happy path is easy. What if the network drops mid-transaction? What if two requests race on the same balance? These questions separate production systems from demos.
